# Finance Education YouTube Channel — Full Launch Plan

**Working title: "Money Autopsy"** — a faceless, cinematic finance-education channel where every
episode is a forensic post-mortem of one real financial life: how the money was made, where it
died, and the exact numbers you can run on yourself.

This plan follows the method from *"I Used Higgsfield AI + Claude to Build a $39,500/Month
Faceless Channel"* (Claude for research/scripts, Higgsfield MCP for visuals + voiceover, no
camera, no team) — but adapts it into an **original, defensible format** rather than a clone,
which matters both for standing out and for staying monetizable under YouTube's 2025+
inauthentic-content rules.

---

## 1. Style selection — what actually resonates in finance on YouTube

| Style | Examples | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Whiteboard animation | The Swedish Investor, WhiteBoard Finance | Cheap, clear, evergreen | Saturated; low production ceiling; weak Shorts crossover |
| Talking-head slides + AI voice | Hundreds of faceless clones | Fastest to produce | Exactly what YouTube now demonetizes as "mass-produced" |
| News/market updates | Various | Built-in demand | Time-decaying content; endless treadmill; compliance risk |
| **Cinematic story-documentary explainer** | MagnatesMedia, How Money Works, Hoser | Highest retention & RPM ($10–$35 in finance), evergreen, premium sponsor appeal | Most production effort — which AI now collapses |

**Selected style: cinematic story-documentary explainer.** It is the proven top performer in
finance (story = retention, education = search value, wealthy audience = premium RPM), and its
one historical downside — production cost — is precisely what the Claude + Higgsfield pipeline
eliminates.

## 2. The original take: "Money Autopsy"

**Premise.** Every episode opens on an outcome (a fortune, a bankruptcy, a quiet $8M estate)
and works backwards like a forensic investigation. A consistent noir "Auditor" character
(Higgsfield Soul ID) walks a stylized records room — ledgers, receipts, evidence boards —
narrating the case.

**The differentiator — "The Ledger" segment.** The final 90 seconds of every episode leaves the
story and puts the *viewer's* numbers on screen: "here is the same math applied to a normal
salary," rendered as clean animated charts. Viewers are sent to a **free companion web app —
the Wealth Dashboard in this repo** — to run their own autopsy. No other cinematic finance
channel closes the loop from story → personal action → owned tool. That is the moat:

- The dashboard is a lead magnet (email list / community later).
- Dashboard screen recordings double as authentic, non-AI b-roll (helps originality review).
- Long-term monetization (premium features, courses) lives on an asset we own, not the algorithm.

**Format signature (repeatable template):**
1. **Cold open (0:00–0:30)** — the outcome, stated as a mystery. "Ronald Read pumped gas his whole life. He died with $8 million. This is the autopsy."
2. **Act 1: The Life (0:30–3:00)** — cinematic story beats.
3. **Act 2: The Evidence (3:00–7:00)** — the 3–4 decisions that actually drove the outcome, each with real numbers on an evidence board.
4. **Act 3: Cause of Wealth/Death (7:00–9:00)** — the verdict, one principle, plainly stated.
5. **The Ledger (9:00–10:30)** — "run it on yourself": animated charts, dashboard link, CTA.

Target length 9–12 minutes (mid-roll eligible). Each episode also yields 2–3 vertical Shorts
(the cold open, one evidence beat, one Ledger stat).

## 6. Compliance & monetization safety (important)

- **YouTube inauthentic-content policy (2025+):** mass-produced, unedited AI content is
  demonetized. Our protection: original research, human-edited scripts, an owned recurring
  character, real dashboard footage, and a unique format. AI is the *production* tool, not the
  *content*.
- **Disclose synthetic media** via YouTube's altered-content checkbox where realistic AI
  visuals are used.
- **Finance disclaimer** in every description and verbally in The Ledger: education, not
  financial advice. Never give personalized recommendations; avoid "guaranteed returns"
  language (also an advertiser-friendliness issue).
- **Real people in Collapse episodes:** stick to well-documented public figures and public
  records; state facts with sources; no defamation, no speculation framed as fact.

## 7. Monetization roadmap

1. **Phase 1 (months 1–3):** none — focus on YPP eligibility (1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours
   or 10M Shorts views). Realistic expectation: the "$39,500/month" in the source video is
   marketing; finance RPMs of $10–35 mean ~100k long-form views/month ≈ $1,000–3,500. Plan
   for a 6–12 month ramp, not 20 minutes.
2. **Phase 2 (months 3–6):** AdSense + affiliates that fit the Ledger segment natively
   (brokerages, budgeting tools, books cited in episodes).
3. **Phase 3 (months 6–12):** sponsorships (finance sponsors pay top-tier CPMs for this
   audience), dashboard email list.
4. **Phase 4 (year 1+):** premium dashboard features / templates / community — revenue on an
   asset we own.

## 9. KPIs and the first 90 days

- **Weeks 1–4 (Ep 1–4):** watch for **30-second hook retention > 70%** and **average view
  duration > 40%** — fix hooks before anything else.
- **Weeks 5–8 (Ep 5–8):** iterate thumbnails/titles for **CTR 4–6%**; double down on the
  best-performing pillar.
- **Weeks 9–13 (Ep 9–12):** push Shorts → long-form conversion; target YPP eligibility;
  measure dashboard clicks per 1k views (north-star for the moat).
- Review cadence: 30 minutes of analytics every Monday; kill or reformat any pillar that
  underperforms two episodes in a row.
